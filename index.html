How to Use a Multimeter Properly
Always start with highest range setting. Never measure current on a live circuit without proper training. One wrong probe placement can destroy your meter or cause injury.
Measuring AC Voltage (Most Common)
Set dial to AC voltage (V~). Insert black probe into COM port, red probe into VŒ© port. Touch probes to hot and neutral (220V expected). Display shows voltage. Never touch metal probe tips while measuring.
Testing Continuity (Wire Integrity)
POWER MUST BE OFF. Set to continuity mode ())) symbol). Touch probes together - meter beeps. Touch probes to both ends of wire - beep means wire is continuous/good. No beep means break/open circuit.
Measuring Resistance (Ohms)
POWER MUST BE OFF. Set to Œ© (ohms). Start at highest range. Touch probes to component. Lower range if reading shows "1" or "OL". Good wire shows near 0Œ©. Infinite resistance means open circuit.
Testing Current (ADVANCED - Use Clamp Meter Instead)
AVOID unless experienced. Requires breaking circuit. Set to A (amps), insert probes into A terminals. Connect meter IN SERIES with load. Start with highest range (10A). Wrong connection can blow fuse or damage meter.
Common Mistakes to Avoid
‚ùå Measuring voltage with probes in A terminals (shorts meter). ‚ùå Measuring resistance on live circuit (destroys meter). ‚ùå Touching probe tips while measuring voltage (shock). ‚ùå Using wrong range (inaccurate reading). ‚úÖ Always verify meter working on known voltage first.
If measuring voltage and display shows nothing or acts erratic, STOP. Check probe connections, battery, and fuse. A faulty meter is dangerous.
How to Identify Wire Types & Gauges
Reading Wire Markings (Printed on Jacket)
Look for: "THHN 12 AWG 600V" or similar. THHN/THWN = insulation type. 12 AWG = wire size. 600V = voltage rating. May also show "90¬∞C" (temperature rating). PH common: THHN, THWN, TW for building wire.
Measuring Unknown Wire Gauge
Strip 10mm of insulation. Use wire stripper gauge holes. Insert bare wire into holes until tight fit. That hole number = wire gauge. Or use digital caliper: 2.05mm = 14 AWG, 3.31mm = 12 AWG, 5.26mm = 10 AWG.
Philippine Color Coding (Standard Practice)
‚ö° HOT/LIVE: Black, Red, Blue (any color except white/green). ‚ö™ NEUTRAL: White or Gray only. üü¢ GROUND: Green, Green/Yellow stripe, or Bare copper. Note: Old wiring may not follow this - always test!
Solid vs Stranded Wire
SOLID (single thick conductor): Used in permanent installations, walls, conduit. Stiffer, easier to push through conduit. STRANDED (many thin strands): Used in cords, flexible applications, vibration areas. More flexible but harder to terminate.
Common PH Wire Sizes & Uses
2.0mm¬≤ (14 AWG): Lighting circuits, 15A max. 3.5mm¬≤ (12 AWG): General outlets, 20A max. 5.5mm¬≤ (10 AWG): Aircon, heavy appliances, 30A. 8.0mm¬≤ (8 AWG): Electric range, large AC, 40A. 14mm¬≤ (6 AWG): Main feeds, 50-60A.
PEC Requirement: Minimum 2.0mm¬≤ (14 AWG) for branch circuits. Never use speaker wire, lamp cord, or undersized wire for permanent installations. Always check ampacity tables in PEC for exact requirements.
Never assume wire color = function in old installations. Always test with multimeter to verify hot, neutral, and ground before working.
Basic Wire Connections & Splicing
POWER OFF and verified before making any connections. Poor connections cause 80% of electrical fires. Take your time and do it right.
Proper Wire Nut Installation
Strip wires same length (12-15mm). Hold wires parallel, twist clockwise 1-2 turns with pliers (pre-twist). Select correct size nut (must fit wire bundle). Thread nut clockwise onto wires, twisting firmly until tight. Tug each wire - should not pull out. No bare copper visible below nut.
Creating Pigtail Connections
When multiple wires connect to one terminal: Cut 150mm piece of same gauge wire. Strip 15mm one end, 10mm other end. Group all wires (including pigtail) together. Twist with wire nut. Connect pigtail's 10mm end to device terminal. This prevents strain on device.
Wire-to-Screw Terminal (CRITICAL TECHNIQUE)
Strip exactly 10mm (not more). Form clockwise hook using needle-nose pliers. Loop should be 3/4 circle. Place loop under screw head going CLOCKWISE direction. Tighten screw - tightening pulls loop closed. No bare wire visible outside terminal. Test with gentle tug.
Using Crimp Connectors
Strip wire per connector specs (usually 10mm). Insert wire fully into connector until hitting stop. Use proper crimping tool (not pliers). Crimp firmly in center of barrel. Tug test - wire should not pull out. Use heat shrink or tape for insulation if not pre-insulated.
Inline Splice for Repairs
Cut out damaged section. Strip 15mm on both ends. Overlap wires and twist together clockwise. Solder if possible (better connection). Cover with wire nut or crimp connector. Wrap with electrical tape. MUST be inside accessible junction box per code - no splices in walls!
NEVER use twist-on wire connectors for aluminum wire without proper compound and AL-rated connectors. Aluminum connections require special techniques to prevent fire hazards.
PEC Splice Requirements: All splices must be in accessible junction boxes. No splices allowed inside walls, ceilings, or inaccessible spaces. Box must have cover. Splices must be mechanically and electrically secure.

Common residential installation tasks

Installing a Wall Outlet (Duplex Receptacle)
Turn OFF circuit breaker and verify with voltage tester. Lock out panel if possible.
Prepare the Outlet Box
Install utility box (2"x4" or 4"x4") at 300mm from floor level. Secure with proper anchors.
Run Wire to Box
Use 3.5mm¬≤ THHN wire (black/hot, white/neutral, green/ground). Leave 150-200mm extra wire in box.
Strip Wire Ends
Strip 10-15mm of insulation. Use wire stripper, not blade to avoid nicking copper.
Connect Wires to Receptacle
Hot (black) ‚Üí brass screw, Neutral (white) ‚Üí silver screw, Ground (green) ‚Üí green screw. Clockwise loops.
Secure Receptacle
Fold wires into box carefully. Screw receptacle to box ears. Install faceplate.
Test Installation
Turn ON breaker. Test with outlet tester or plug-in device. Check for proper grounding.
Installing a Light Switch
Ensure power is OFF at circuit breaker. Use non-contact voltage tester to verify.
Mount Switch Box
Install at 1400mm height (standard). Ensure box is securely mounted and level.
Identify Wire Functions
Line (power from panel), Load (to light), Neutral (if smart switch), Ground.
Connect Line Wire
Connect hot wire from panel to common or LINE terminal (usually brass or marked).
Connect Load Wire
Connect wire going to light fixture to switched terminal on switch.
Secure and Test
Pigtail grounds together. Mount switch, install plate. Restore power and test operation.
Installing Circuit Breaker Panel
This task requires PRC Licensed Electrical Engineer supervision and RME execution. Never attempt without proper credentials.
Coordinate with Utility
Request disconnection from Meralco. Obtain approval for meter base and panel installation.
Mount Panel Enclosure
Install at 1500-1800mm height. Ensure adequate clearance (min 1000mm in front, 200mm sides).
Install Main Breaker
Size appropriately for service (typically 60-100A residential). Connect to main lugs.
Install Branch Breakers
15A for lighting, 20A for outlets, 30-40A for aircon, sized accordingly for appliances.
Ground and Bond
Connect ground bar to grounding electrode system. Bond neutral bar at main panel only.
Label Circuits
Clearly mark each breaker with location and load. Use permanent labels. Update as-built drawings.
Inspection and Energization
Schedule LGU electrical inspection. Obtain CFEI. Coordinate utility connection.
Troubleshooting Tripped Breakers
Identify Affected Circuit
Check panel for tripped breaker (handle in middle position or OFF). Note which circuit.
Unplug All Devices
Disconnect all appliances and devices on the affected circuit.
Reset Breaker
Push breaker fully OFF, then ON. If trips immediately, likely short circuit or ground fault.
Test Devices One by One
Plug in devices individually. If breaker trips, faulty device identified.
Check for Overload
Calculate total wattage. Ensure not exceeding circuit capacity (220V x Breaker Amps x 0.8).
Inspect for Damage
If persistent tripping, inspect outlets and wiring for damage. Call licensed electrician if needed.
Wire Stripping & Termination ESSENTIAL
Properly remove insulation without nicking conductor. Create proper loops for screw terminals. Use crimp connectors correctly. Master both solid and stranded wire techniques.
Circuit Testing & Measurement ESSENTIAL
Use multimeter to measure voltage, current, resistance. Test continuity. Identify hot, neutral, ground. Verify proper grounding. Detect open circuits and shorts.
Load Calculation REQUIRED
Calculate circuit loads using P = V √ó I formula. Determine proper wire gauge for load. Size circuit breakers correctly. Plan panel capacity. Apply NEC/PEC demand factors.
Conduit Installation INTERMEDIATE
Bend EMT/PVC conduit properly. Calculate conduit fill capacity. Install junction boxes correctly. Maintain proper support spacing. Create weatherproof outdoor installations.
Panel Installation & Modification ADVANCED
Install main service panels. Add branch circuits. Balance panel loads. Upgrade service capacity. Properly bond and ground systems. Must have PRC-EE supervision.
Grounding & Bonding Systems ADVANCED
Install ground rods properly (min 2.4m deep). Size grounding conductors. Bond metal components. Install GFCI protection. Create equipotential bonding for swimming pools.
Smart Home Integration EMERGING
Install smart switches requiring neutral wires. Set up home automation systems. Configure wireless controls. Integrate with existing electrical systems. Future-proof installations.
Solar PV Systems SPECIALIZED
Install solar panels and inverters. Connect to grid (Meralco net metering). Size battery systems. Understand DC vs AC systems. Comply with solar-specific PEC requirements.

Lockout/Tagout Procedure

Identify Power Source
Locate correct circuit breaker controlling work area
Notify Affected Parties
Inform household members work is being performed
Turn OFF and Lock
Switch breaker OFF. Use lock or tape to prevent accidental re-energization
Verify Zero Voltage
Use voltage tester on ALL wires. Test hot-to-neutral, hot-to-ground, neutral-to-ground
Perform Work Safely
Keep one hand in pocket when testing. Work methodically. Double-check connections
Re-energize Properly
Remove locks/tags. Turn ON breaker. Test circuit functionality. Verify proper operation

Working in Wet Conditions

NEVER work on electrical systems during rain, in standing water, or with wet hands. Water drastically reduces electrical resistance and increases shock risk.
Wait for dry conditions. Use GFCI protection. Wear rubber boots. Keep tools dry. If emergency repair needed, call qualified electrician.

Electrical Shock Response

DO NOT Touch Victim
If still in contact with electricity, touching them will shock you too
Cut Power Source
Turn OFF breaker or disconnect at main panel. Use non-conductive object to move victim if necessary
Call Emergency Services
Dial 911 or local emergency number immediately. Every second counts
Begin CPR if Trained
Check breathing and pulse. Start chest compressions if no heartbeat. Continue until help arrives

Arc Flash Hazards

Arc flash can cause severe burns, blindness, and death. Temperature can reach 35,000¬∞F. Always assume energized equipment can arc.
Maintain proper working distance from energized parts
Wear arc-rated PPE for panel work (FR clothing)
Never use metallic rulers or tools near live circuits
Keep face away from panel when turning ON breakers
De-energize equipment before working whenever possible

Fire Safety

Recognize Warning Signs
Burning smell, discolored outlets, warm switches, flickering lights, buzzing sounds
Cut Power Immediately
Turn OFF circuit breaker. If fire visible, turn OFF main breaker
Use Proper Extinguisher
Class C (electrical fires) or ABC multi-purpose. NEVER use water on electrical fires
Evacuate if Necessary
If fire spreads or power cannot be cut, evacuate immediately and call fire department
Always work with a buddy for major electrical projects. Second person can call for help, operate breakers, or provide emergency assistance if needed.

Tool Maintenance

Inspect Before Each Use
Check insulation integrity. Look for cracks, exposed metal. Test voltage testers daily
Clean Regularly
Wipe tools after use. Remove dust and debris. Keep cutting edges sharp
Calibrate Test Equipment
Verify multimeter accuracy annually. Test against known voltage source. Replace batteries
Store Properly
Keep tools dry. Organized toolbox or bag. Separate insulated from regular tools
Replace any tool with damaged insulation immediately. Compromised insulation can conduct electricity and cause serious injury or death.
